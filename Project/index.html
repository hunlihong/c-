    What is Information Technology?

    An information technology (IT) major is a degree program that focuses on the study of computers, information systems, and telecommunications. IT majors learn how to design, develop, and manage information systems that support business operations. They also learn about the ethical and legal issues related to the use of technology.

    Why should be IT?

      There are some reasons that IT should be chosen to study as a major nowadays:
    • High demand: The demand for IT professionals is high and is expected to continue to grow in the coming years. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of computer and information technology occupations is projected to grow 13% from 2020 to 2030, much faster than the average for all occupations.
    • Variety of career options: There are many different IT careers to choose from, so you can find one that is a good fit for your skills and interests. Some of the most popular IT careers include software developer, systems administrator, network engineer, IT security analyst, and database administrator.
    • Good salary: IT professionals typically earn a good salary. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for computer and information technology occupations was $91,250 in May 2020.
    • Job security: IT jobs are relatively recession-proof. This is because businesses need IT professionals to keep their systems running and their data secure, even during economic downturns.
    • Upward mobility: There are many opportunities for advancement in the IT field. As you gain experience and skills, you can move into more senior positions with higher salaries and responsibilities.

    Computer Science

    A computer science major is a degree program that focuses on the theoretical and practical aspects of computing. Computer science majors learn about computer hardware, software, and algorithms. They also learn about the principles of problem-solving and how to design and implement solutions using computers. A computer science major can be a great choice for people who are interested in technology, problem-solving, and creativity. Computer science majors have a variety of career options available to them, including software development, data science, artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity.

    Software Engineering/Development

    Software engineering is the application of engineering principles to the design, development, testing, and maintenance of software. It is a broad field that encompasses many different aspects of software development, including:

    • Requirements engineering: Identifying the needs of the software users and stakeholders.
    • Software design: Creating a blueprint for the software system.
    • Software development: Coding the software system.
    • Software testing: Finding and fixing errors in the software system.
    • Software maintenance: Keeping the software system up-to-date and fixing bugs.

    Computer Networking and Cyber Security

    Computer networking is the study of how computers communicate with each other. It encompasses the design, construction, and operation of computer networks, as well as the protocols and technologies that allow computers to communicate.


      Cyber security is the practice of protecting computer systems and networks from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction. It includes a wide range of activities, such as:
    • Preventing cyberattacks: This involves identifying and mitigating vulnerabilities in computer systems and networks.
    • Detecting cyberattacks: This involves monitoring computer systems and networks for signs of unauthorized activity.

    Computer Multimedia Design

      Computer multimedia design is the art of integrating different media, such as text, images, audio, and video to create interactive experiences. It is a rapidly growing field, with applications in a wide range of industries, including:
    • Education: Multimedia design is used to create educational materials that are engaging and interactive.
    • Marketing: Multimedia design is used to create marketing materials that are persuasive and memorable.
    • Entertainment: Multimedia design is used to create games, movies, and other forms of entertainment.
    • Business: Multimedia design is used to create corporate presentations, websites, and other materials that communicate a company's message.
    • Healthcare: Multimedia design is used to create educational materials about health and wellness.

    Digital Marketing

      Digital marketing is the use of digital channels to reach and engage with customers. It includes a wide range of activities, such as:
    • Search engine optimization (SEO): This is the process of improving the visibility of a website in search engine results pages (SERPs).
    • Pay-per-click (PPC) advertising: This is the process of paying for ads to appear on search engine results pages or other websites.
    • Content marketing: This is the process of creating and sharing valuable content to attract and engage customers.
    • Social media marketing: This is the process of using social media platforms to connect with and engage with customers.
    • Email marketing: This is the process of sending email messages to customers to promote products or services.
    • Display advertising: This is the process of displaying ads on websites or other digital platforms.
    • Affiliate marketing: This is the process of earning commissions by promoting other businesses' products or services.
    • Influencer marketing: This is the process of partnering with influencers to promote products or services to their followers.
